# All Book Prompts

Every example AI prompt from the book, organized by chapter. Copy any of these into your AI tool of choice, replacing the bracketed placeholders with your own details.

---

## Chapter 1 — Foundations

**Problem first:**
*"I'm a new PM at a health app working with a senior engineering lead who reaches for solutions early. Write a short message that brings us back to the problem, so the team can weigh a few approaches before committing."*

**Strategy:**
*"Here's a draft mission statement for a product: Pulse. Does it answer why we exist, or does it read more like a vision? What's missing?"*

**Key results:**
*"Here's an objective: increase user engagement. Write three key results that would tell me whether it's actually working, then tell me what's weak about the objective itself."*

**Decisions:**
*"I need to decide whether to add a social sharing feature to a health tracking app. What discovery questions should I answer before committing to build it?"*

---

## Chapter 2 — Strategy

**Vision:**
*"Here's a draft vision statement for a health tracking app: Pulse. Does it describe a product or an outcome? Rewrite it three ways that describe the future for the user, not the features of the app."*

**Segmentation:**
*"I'm building a health tracking app. Generate five distinct user segments based on behavioural differences, not demographics. For each, describe the primary job they'd hire this app to do."*

**Positioning:**
*"Here are three competing health apps: [list them]. What positioning spaces are they occupying? Where is there a gap a new entrant could credibly win?"*

---

## Chapter 3 — Discovery

**On problem discovery:**
*"I'm researching why people abandon health tracking apps. Generate ten open-ended interview questions that get at the reasons behind their behaviour without leading them toward a particular answer."*

**On experiment design:**
*"Here's my hypothesis: [type yours]. What's the riskiest assumption inside it, and what's the cheapest experiment I could run to test that assumption?"*

**On synthesis:**
*"Here are five interview transcripts. [paste them] What themes appear across more than one conversation? Quote the specific lines that support each theme. Don't invent new themes."*

---

## Chapter 4 — Prioritization

*"Here's a Pulse backlog item: [describe it]. Walk through the four RICE inputs and tell me what I'd need to know to estimate each one honestly."*

*"I scored this feature high on impact but I'm not confident. What are three cheap ways I could raise or lower my confidence before committing?"*

*"Here's my MoSCoW list. [enter it] Challenge my 'Must' bucket. Which of these would the product actually survive without?"*

---

## Chapter 5 — Requirements, Handoff & Prototyping

**Drafting requirements:**
*"Here's a job a user is trying to do: [describe it]. Write three user stories with testable acceptance criteria. Make each criterion something I could mark true or false."*

**Building the prototype:**
*"Here's my PRD for a Pulse feature: [describe it]. Build a working prototype that follows it. Ask me questions about my PRD before you begin where it's ambiguous."*

**Testing:**
*"Here's my feature and its guardrails: [describe them]. Generate ten test cases, including adversarial ones that try to make it violate a guardrail."*

**Making a PRD:**
*"I want to make a human and AI ready product requirements document for [feature or product]. Interview me and when you have all responses, generate a prd.md file."*

---

## Chapter 6 — Execution

**Choosing a method:**
*"Here's a description of a piece of work: [describe it]. Walk me through whether Scrum or Kanban fits better, and what would make me choose one over the other."*

**Decision rights:**
*"Here's a cross-functional decision we're stuck on: [describe it]. Help me draft a DACI: who should be the single Approver, and who are contributors versus informed?"*

**Roadmaps:**
*"Here's a feature list with dates. Help me turn it into a roadmap that shows what we'll ship, roughly when (by quarter), and why, with each feature tied to the objective it serves."*

---

## Chapter 7 — Go-to-Market

**Launch comms:**
*"Here's what our feature does: [describe it]. Draft a short launch announcement and five FAQ entries. Flag any claim that I'd need to verify before publishing."*

**Support readiness:**
*"Here's our new feature and its known limitations: [describe them]. Write five support macros for the most likely user problems, in a warm, plain tone."*

**Launch plan:**
*"Here's our launch: [describe audience and feature]. What cross-functional readiness am I likely forgetting across marketing, support, and operations?"*

---

## Chapter 8 — Post-Launch Management

**Reading your data:**
*"Here's weekly data for a feature: [describe it]. What story does it tell about whether this feature is growing, mature, or declining, and what would you watch next?"*

**Maintain/improve/retire call:**
*"Here's a feature's success metric and its actual performance: [describe both]. Make the case for maintaining it, the case for improving it, and the case for retiring it."*

**Monitoring an AI feature:**
*"Here's an AI feature and its guardrails: [describe them]. What behavioural signals, not just usage, should I monitor in production to catch it drifting?"*
