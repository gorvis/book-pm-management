# DACI: Roles and a Pulse Example

DACI assigns four roles to any significant decision, so it's clear who runs it, who decides, who informs it, and who just needs to know the outcome. The table below pairs each role's definition with how it plays out for a _Pulse_ decision: which market to launch the weekly summary in first.

| Role | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Driver** | Runs the decision: gathers input, frames the options, keeps it moving | The product manager, framing the launch-market options and running the decision |
| **Approver** | The single person who makes the final call | The product sponsor (e.g., Product Director) |
| **Contributors** | Inform the decision with expertise or context | Marketing, engineering, and support, each informing with what they know |
| **Informed** | Told the outcome, but not part of making it | Other product teams at the company, told the outcome so they can plan around it |
